AI and Information Systems in Business

AI's role in Information Systems

AI's role in information systems in short is to enhance the capabilities of human decision makers, automate repetitive and complex tasks, and provide insights and predictions from large and diverse data sources.

How these roles are fulfilled:

Enhancing capabilities of human decision-making: AI helps decision makers make better decisions faster by providing relevant and reliable information as well as suggestions and possible explanations. For example, A doctor may enter in a patient's symptoms the AI will then take this data along with other data (such as the patient's medical history) it will then suggest possible causes and treatments which could help the doctor come to a diagnosis faster.


Automate repetitive and complex tasks: AI automates tasks which are tedious, time-consuming, or error-prone, by performing them more efficiently and accurately. A good example of such would be in an accounting firm the AI system can scan invoices, receipts, and bank statements it will then extract the relevant information such as amounts, dates, and categories. The AI then automatically update the accounting records and generate reports and insights. This saves tremendous time, reduces errors, and improves accuracy.


Provide insights and predictions: AI provides insights and predictions from data sets which are too large or complex for human analysis, by applying advanced techniques such as machine learning, data mining, and statistical modelling. For example, the AI can determine when the best time to sell strawberry flavoured ice-cream is based off data from customers spending habits on other products.


Examples of practical applications of future trends of AI in Information Systems:

Natural language processing (NLP): This is the branch of AI that deals with the analysis and generation of natural language, such as text and speech. NLP can be used to create chatbots, voice assistants, sentiment analysis, text summarization, machine translation, and more. NLP with information systems allows users to interact with the data in a more intuitive way as they could ask for the data to be compared to other sources and would not have to think of the language barrier.


Computer vision: This is the branch of AI that deals with the processing and understanding of visual information, such as images and videos. Computer vision can be used to create facial recognition, object detection, scene understanding, image editing, etc. This makes it much easier to input various types of data from invoices to counting stock on hand. It also makes displaying the data in various graph much easier and faster.


Machine learning: This is the branch of AI that deals with the creation and application of algorithms which learn from data and improve their performance over time. Machine learning can be used to create recommendation systems, anomaly detection, fraud detection, spam filtering, and many more. This technology enables companies to optimise their strategies and maximise sales by finding various patterns and trends from their recorded data.
